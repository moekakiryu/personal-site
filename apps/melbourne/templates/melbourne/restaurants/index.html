Restaurant Recommendations

So, there's just too much good food in Melbourne to narrow it down to a handful of 'best' spots, so instead I made an list of all of my favourite places.

Hopefully this helps narrow things down a little!!

Legend

{% include "melbourne/icons/star.svg" %} Must try Places marked must-try are either just exceptionally good, represent quintessential Melbourne, or both. Basically, these are fun places with good food.

{% include "melbourne/icons/timer-short.svg" %} Small wait expected Many places in Melbourne have queues to get in the door and these are no exception. These places tend to move people through quickly, but larger groups may have a bit of a wait during busy times.

{% include "melbourne/icons/timer-long.svg" %} Long wait expected These places are either very popular right now, or have very small venues. Small groups may have to wait >20-30min and larger groups may have to wait longer. I definitely recommend trying to hit these closer to when they open (or any time before 10:30ish for brunches) to avoid the crowds.

{% include "melbourne/icons/calendar.svg" %} Booking recommended These places do accept walk-ins (even for moderate-large groups), however still get busy enough during peak hours that a booking can help a lot. However, if a booking is too much of a hassle, they will still do their best to squeeze you in.

{% include "melbourne/icons/calendar.svg" %} Booking required These places either do not accept walk-ins or are so busy you will not get a seat without a booking. Try to book as far in advance for these restaurants.

{% include "melbourne/icons/umbrella.svg" %} No indoor seating These places are either takeaway only or don't have much space inside, so plan on bringing a jacket when you visit. Note some of these have no seats at all, while others just don't have protection from the elements.
